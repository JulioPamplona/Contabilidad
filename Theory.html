PRIMO COST:

The prime cost is defined as all the direct costs of a product, that is,
those costs that can be tracked conveniently for each unit produced.
It then refers to the cost of direct materials and the cost of direct labor.

PRODUCTION COST:

Production costs (also called operating costs) are the necessary expenses for
keep a project, processing line or equipment running. In a standard company,
the difference between income (from sales and other income) and the cost of production indicates gross profit.

INDIRECT COSTS:

It is the sum of those expenses necessary in the preparation of a product, obtaining a job or service.

COST OF DISTRIBUTION:

The distribution costs are all the expenses incurred by a producer to deliver their products,
from its manufacturing plant to the place where they are acquired, thus constituting a component
of the total cost to the producer and the price paid by consumers, therefore their analysis
It is very important in terms of business efficiency.

TOTAL COST OF OPERATION:

They are the expenses associated with the operation of a company on a day-to-day basis. Expenses include all
The costs to operate, but in general can be divided into two main categories: maintenance and administration.

UNITARY PRODUCTION COST:

It is a measure of the total costs and expenses necessary to produce, shelter, transport and sell a unit
of a particular product or service.
